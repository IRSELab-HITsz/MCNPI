Motion Control Framework for Humanoid Robots Based on Non-Privileged Information

Shilong Sun1,2, Yuanpeng Wang1, Haodong Huang1, Zida Zhao1, Chiyao Li1 Wenfu Xu1,2,3
1School of Robotics and Advanced Manufacturing, Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen , 2Guangdong Provincial Key Laboratory of Intelligent Morphing Mechanisms and Adaptive Robotics 3Key University Laboratory of Mechanism & Machine Theory and Intelligent Unmanned Systems of Guangdong
Paper Video Code

Non-Privileged Information and Reinforcement Learning.

Abstract

Recent research in humanoid robot motion control has made significant progress, particularly with the capabilities of reinforcement learning methods in complex environments. However, existing studies often focus on the design of privileged information and reward functions, a process that is typically time-consuming and requires careful selection of reward parameters to develop an efficient motion control framework. This paper presents a novel motion control framework for humanoid robots that relies solely on limited self-perception data, enabling humanoid robots to walk on challenging terrains without depending on privileged information. To validate the effectiveness of our approach, we conducted simulation experiments. The results demonstrate that our control framework outperforms existing methods, thereby advancing the development and application of humanoid robots in complex environments.
